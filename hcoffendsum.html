Meet the Haters

The white racial cohort commits the largest number of domestic hate crimes. According to the Department of Justice, perpetrators who identified as being white committed 34 percent of the hate crimes between 2013 and 2015. That said, the white racial cohort makes up 72.4 percent of the U.S. population according to the 2010 U.S. Census. If you are a pessimist this suggests there is room to grow.

The second largest group of haters are “Unknown Offenders”. This group represents hate crime perpetrators that were not identified by their victims or the police. This group committed 21 percent of the hate crime offenses between 2013 and 2015.

The Hispanic and Latino ethnic cohort commit fewer hate crimes relative to their representation in the U.S. population. Hispanics and Latinos make up 16 percent of the U.S. population but only commit 8 percent of the hate crimes. From a hate crime perspective, there are relatively few “bad hombres” in this population cohort.
